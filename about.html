Driven to create positive change.

We are art enthusiasts and providers of art enrichment to all children including those with special needs. We believe everyone has an artist inside looking to express themselves, and we want to help your child find their artistic voice.

What is CogniDoodle?

CogniDoodle is an inclusion program (a setting where children with special needs work together with typical peers) that aims to strengthen and develop cognitive, language, and social skills through art. We offer process-based art enrichment where self-expression is the goal. This allows all students to improve their critical-thinking and communication skills without worrying about their artistic talent.

Why we do it?

Well, we were just like you. "What do you mean?" I'm glad you asked.
We were looking for programs and activities that would be fun and functional for our kiddos. Something that would expose them to new experiences while being inclusive. In our, search we kept coming across programs that were great but also exclusive—separating children with disabilities and typical children. However, research shows that there are benefits for children with and without disabilities in inclusion programs. We couldn't find what we were seeking. So being good moms, we created our own.

So, Who's behind this?

Our founders Tristyn and Laurelle, of course.  They are proud mothers to loving, caring, talented, and creative kids.  Fusing each founders' education and passion, CogniDoodle aims to offer more programming for families with children of different needs.

Tristyn

Tristyn has 9 years of experience providing support in schools and clinical settings. She has a background in speech and language pathology. She is currently pursuing her Master's in Counseling. In these settings, she served as a member of the Assistive Technology Committee and Coordinator for her school's Autism Support Team. Tristyn spends her free time baking with her son, gardening, crafting, and advocating for families with special needs.

Laurelle

Laurelle is a professional illustrator and designer. With a background in Science and Fine Arts, she has experience working in elementary schools teaching Art, STEM, and Robotics. Her specialties include pen and ink, colored pencil, and acrylic media. Laurelle enjoys coloring with her children, working as a makeup artist, painting, crocheting, and volunteering as a Girl Scout troop co-leader.
